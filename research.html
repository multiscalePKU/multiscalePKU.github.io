AI for multiscale modeling

Artificial intelligence (AI) has been a great success in many applications, followed by the recent wave of using AI techniques for solving scientific problems, such as molecular dynamics, reactive flow, etc. Our group focuses on the development of powerful multiscale modeling methods and algorithms based on machine learning techniques. Currently, our focuses include metmaterials , Lithium batteries, granular materials, plasticity, among others. Our group is in close collaboration with AI for Science Institute and DP technology.

Computational multiphyics

Multiple physiochemical processes, such as deformation, heat/mass transfer, chemical reaction, are often coupled in engineering problems. More often than not, these processes take place in complex geometries and can cross very disparate spatio-temporal scales. In our group, we aim at developing advanced computational methods to efficiently solve these problems, in an attempt to guide the design, optimization and even control of problems such as fracture mechanics, electrochemistry, phase separation and phase transition, etc.

Soft and living matter

Soft and living matter can be fascinatingly complex, yet works under extremely simple principles. Our group construct minimal physics-based models to unravel such simple principles behind the complexity of the system. In particular, our research focuses on biomolecular condensates, nonequilibrium thermodynamics, bacteria and biofilms, collective behavior of social networks, etc.
